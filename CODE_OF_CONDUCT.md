# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free environment for everyone who participates in the BitChaser project.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of positive behavior include:

* Communicating respectfully and constructively
* Welcoming questions and contributions
* Giving and accepting useful technical feedback
* Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
* Focusing discussions on the project and its goals
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and working to correct them

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

* Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
* Personal attacks, insults, or deliberately inflammatory comments
* Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention
* Publishing another person's private information without permission
* Repeated disruption of project discussions
* Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional environment

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all BitChaser project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, and other project-related communication.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in a public space.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may temporarily or permanently restrict participation by individuals whose conduct is considered inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Reporting

Conduct concerns should be reported privately to the project maintainer through an appropriate private contact method.

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as reasonably possible. The privacy and safety of the reporter will be respected.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is informed by the Contributor Covenant and other commonly used open-source community standards.
